Hurricanes - Basic Facts

  • Caused by sun heating ocean water which evaporates with rising warm air into swirling wind patterns
  • Winds swirl in a counter-clockwise rotation in the Earth’s Northern hemisphere and a clockwise rotation in the Southern hemisphere
  • Sustained winds range from 75 mph to over 150
  • Well-formed hurricanes have an “eye” of calm weather in the center
  • “Storm surge” is the term describing the flood waters pushed inland from the ocean by the strong winds
